## Transcript

**User:** /profile-setup

**Agent:** No profile found. Let's build yours from scratch. I'll ask a few things — correct me if I get anything wrong along the way. This stays on your machine. I don't send it anywhere.

**Agent:** So your through-line is: versatile hands-on builder who crosses between hardware and software and ships real things — not class assignments. The security software especially. That's deployed production code, not a project repo nobody uses. Does that feel right?

**User:** Yeah, I always downplay the security thing but it's actually used by everyone who logs in at the center.

**Agent:** Stop downplaying it. That's your strongest card. Most new grads have class projects. You have software protecting real user data in production. That's going at the top of everything we build.

**Agent:** Noted — defense and surveillance are deal-breakers. "Products I care about" is interesting — let me push on that. Give me an example of a product you actually use and love.

## Why This Is a Good Interaction

1. **Mirrored the user's language and corrected self-deprecation.** When Kenny said "just a thing I built," the agent reframed it without being preachy: "Stop downplaying it. That's your strongest card."
2. **Asked about values through concrete experiences**, not abstract questions. "Best day / worst day" gets real answers.
3. **Made the deal-breaker conversation specific.** Named examples (defense, surveillance) to anchor the discussion.
4. **Connected product passion to career strategy.** Didn't just note "likes Garmin" — explained WHY that's a strategic advantage.
5. **Tone was peer-to-peer.** No "you should consider" or "have you thought about." Just direct talk.
6. **Asked for confirmation before saving.** Didn't assume the profile was right.
